  • No dashboard asks how Mum's knee actually feels
  • No automated alert completes the first wellness check
  • Caregivers face constant worry and check-in fatigue
How It Works

KinKeep closes the care loop—with human gates.

1
Notice Change
Passive signals check against her personal historical baseline.
2
Check In First
A friendly, structured conversation starts in her preferred language.
3
Routine Care
KinKeep asks structured questions, records answers, and prepares the next step.
4
One Decision
The family is interrupted only to approve high-impact care steps.

Sovereignty Boundaries

The Agent Stops and Asks

Routine Tasks Agents May Execute Consequential Actions Humans Control
Agent

Read wearable metrics, detect baseline deviation, check in on parent symptoms, draft summaries.

 Human Gate

Choosing a family contact, sharing check-in details, approving a care plan, and deciding whether emergency help is needed.
*Deterministic rules govern the gates. Generative models only compose tone and translations.
"Most agent demos compete on how far autonomy can go. In family care, trust comes from proving exactly where autonomy stops."
— KinKeep Design Principle
OpenAI Build Week Contribution

Built with Codex. Governed by Human Judgment.

  • A complete Care Episode Loop
    Turns a personal-baseline change into a check-in, one family decision, accountable tasks, and a recorded follow-up.
  • Consent and human gates as architecture
    Typed deterministic rules control contact, sharing, approvals, escalation, owners, and deadlines; models cannot bypass them.
  • GPT-5.6 and voice under clear boundaries
